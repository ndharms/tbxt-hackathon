## G site

> Pocket D, which is consistent with the location described as "right in the DNA binding site," is a buried cavity near the N-terminus of the protein that is induced or significantly altered by the G177D mutation. Although it is not directly at the DNA interface, it is in a critical region for protein-protein interactions with chromatin remodelers. Its high druggability score and the fact that it can be targeted by fragments (as shown in the 5QS6 structure with bound ligand K0G) make it a prime site for allosteric inhibition, effectively disrupting brachyury's oncogenic function. Therefore, your "G site" corresponds to the therapeutically promising Pocket D.
